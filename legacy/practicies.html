Green Practices

Conduct a Home Energy Audit: Start by evaluating your home’s energy consumption. Consider reaching out to your city’s energy company or hiring a professional home energy auditor. They can help identify areas for improvement and suggest energy-saving strategies

Seal and Insulate Your Home: Proper insulation and sealing can prevent air leaks through windows, walls, roofs, doors, and cracks. This not only maintains a comfortable indoor temperature but also reduces energy waste and your carbon footprint

Upgrade to Energy-Efficient Appliances: Replace old appliances with energy-efficient models. Look for the ENERGY STAR label, which indicates appliances that meet strict energy efficiency guidelines

Install Programmable Thermostats: Smart thermostats adjust the temperature based on your schedule, reducing energy use when you’re away or asleep

Switch to LED Bulbs: LED bulbs consume less energy and last longer than traditional incandescent bulbs. They are an easy and cost-effective way to reduce electricity usage
